2 /************************************************************************
3  * z C math library
4  * function ZEROIN - obtain a function zero within the given range
5  *
6  * Input
7  * double zeroin(ax,bx,f,tol)
8  * double ax; Root will be seeked for within
9  * double bx; a range [ax,bx]
10  * double f(double x); Name of the function whose zero
11  * will be seeked for
12  * double tol; Acceptable tolerance for the root
13  * value.
14  * May be specified as 0.0 to cause
15  * the program to find the root as
16  * accurate as possible
17  *
18  * Output
19  * Zeroin returns an estimate for the root with accuracy
20  * 4*EPSILON*abs(x) + tol
21  *
22  * Algorithm
23  * G.Forsythe, M.Malcolm, C.Moler, Computer methods for mathematical
24  * computations. M., Mir, 1980, p.180 of the Russian edition
25  *
26  * The function makes use of the bissection procedure combined with
27  * the linear or quadric inverse interpolation.
28  * At every step program operates on three abscissae - a, b, and c.
29  * b - the last and the best approximation to the root
30  * a - the last but one approximation
31  * c - the last but one or even earlier approximation than a that
32  * 1) |f(b)| <= |f(c)|
33  * 2) f(b) and f(c) have opposite signs, i.e. b and c confine
34  * the root
35  * At every step Zeroin selects one of the two new approximations, the
36  * former being obtained by the bissection procedure and the latter
37  * resulting in the interpolation (if a,b, and c are all different
38  * the quadric interpolation is utilized, otherwise the linear one).
39  * If the latter (i.e. obtained by the interpolation) point is
40  * reasonable (i.e. lies within the current interval [b,c] not being
41  * too close to the boundaries) it is accepted. The bissection result
42  * is used in the other case. Therefore, the range of uncertainty is
43  * ensured to be reduced at least by the factor 1.6
44  *
45  ************************************************************************/
